# Technical Assessment - Bond Data Quality Analysis

## Overview

You've been provided with an Excel file (`bond_data.xlsx`) containing green bond issuance data across four tabs:
- **issuances** - Bond-level metadata and summary figures
- **cat_allocations** - Category-level allocation details
- **impacts** - Environmental and social impact metrics
- **geo_allocations** - Geographic distribution of proceeds

Your task is to review this dataset and identify data quality issues, inconsistencies, and anomalies.

---

## Instructions

1. **Identify issues** - Find errors, discrepancies, and values that warrant further investigation. For each issue you identify:
   - Describe what you found
   - Explain why it's problematic or suspicious
   - Suggest what the correct value might be (if determinable) or what additional information you'd need

2. **Prioritize** - Not all issues are equally important. Indicate which findings would be most critical to address and why.

---

## What We're Looking For

This assessment isn't just about finding errors - we want to understand how you work through unfamiliar data. A candidate who finds fewer issues but demonstrates clear thinking and explains *why* something looks wrong is more valuable than one who produces a long list without context.

We're particularly interested in:
- How you validate data across related tables
- Whether you apply domain knowledge or external benchmarks to sanity-check values
- How you distinguish between "technically incorrect" and "worth investigating"

---

## Submission

Please submit:
1. A written summary of your findings (any format - document, markdown, slides)
2. Any code or queries you used (if applicable)

**Time guidance**: We expect this to take approximately 1-1.5 hours. Please don't spend significantly longer - we'd rather see what you prioritize in limited time than an exhaustive audit.

We will ask you to walk through your findings and reasoning in a follow-up conversation.
